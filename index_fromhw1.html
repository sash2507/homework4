A Little Corner of Brooklyn

Posted Today

Ten years ago, I moved from Minnesota to a little corner of Brooklyn called Cobble Hill, an historic italian neighborhood in south-east Brooklyn, just across the Brooklyn and Manhattan bridges. Cobble Hill is a community of small, local shops, cafes, bakeries, bars, brownstones and leafy streets with lots of old-world charm. This webpage shows what I love most about my neighborhood. Come hang out with me sometime!

The Perfect Cobble Hill Sunday

  • 9am: Start your day with a bagel and some light reading at Konditori along Smith st
  • 10am: Stroll over to newly opened Piers 5 and 6 in Brooklyn Bridge Park
  • Noon: Take a leisurely stroll along Atlantic Ave and check out its many, many antique shops and trendy little boutiques
  • 2pm: Head over to French Louie's for a lovely seafood lunch
  • 4pm: Tuck into Bergen Street art gallery Invisible Dog
  • 5pm: It's cocktail hour! Head to Leyenda along Smith St for Latin-themed drinks and snacks
  • 7pm: No Cobble Hill day is complete without a visit to the neighborhood's independent movie theatre, one of the few remaining ones of its kind in NYC!
